# streaming-economics-infrastructure-trade-offs
Understand how infrastructure cost + churn + pricing define business viability.

🎯 Objective

Understand how infrastructure cost + churn + pricing define business viability.

🧠 Context

Platform characteristics:

+ 900k MAUs

+ 7M playbacks/month

+ 50k VOD catalog

+ 120+ live channels

+ No 4K (HD 720p /SD 480p only)

+ ~35 subscription plans offers available

+ High churn (9–12%)

+ CDN-based delivery

💰 Cost Structure (Estimated)

Engineering:

+ Backend + BFF + Frontend squads: ~R$ 12M – 20M / year

Cloud & Backend Infra:

+ APIs, ingestion, storage: ~R$ 8M – 18M / year

CDN (Largest Cost Driver):

+ Video delivery (HD streaming): ~R$ 25M – 60M / year

Data & Analytics

+ Pipelines, tracking, BI: ~R$ 5M – 12M / year

Total Estimated Cost

👉 R$ 54.5M – 108M / year

📊 Unit Economics

+ Cost per User: ~R$ 7–10 per user/month (infra only)

LTV Drivers

+ Price per plan
+ Churn rate
+ Retention curve

🔥 Key Trade-offs

1. Improve Quality (CDN / Player)

     ↓ churn
     ↑ cost

2. Increase Acquisition

     ↑ revenue
     ↑ infra cost

3. Push Bundle (Família Plan)

     ↑ LTV
     ↑ content/licensing cost

📈 Strategic Simulations

Examples:

Reduce churn by 2pp → +25% LTV
Increase CAC → check payback viability
Shift plan mix → impact margin

💡 Key Insight

Growth is constrained not only by marketing efficiency,
but by infrastructure economics.

🎯 Business Implications

Retention improvements may outperform acquisition scaling
Not all growth is profitable
Infrastructure cost must be part of marketing decisions

🔗 How the 3 Cases Connect

Case	Focus	Role
Case 1	Core Models	Decision foundation
Case 2	Causality	Validate impact
Case 3	Economics	Ensure sustainability

🚀 Final Takeaways

Marketing Science is not just modeling → it's decision systems
Retention, acquisition, and infra are interdependent
The best insights come from connecting layers, not isolating them
